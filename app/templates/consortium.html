Mission

UAVCAN is found in manned and unmanned aerial vehicles, spacecraft, robotic transport, and other bleeding-edge vehicular applications dependent on robust means of real-time distributed intravehicular computing.

Being an open project, UAVCAN is critically dependent on direct contributions from its adopters. This Consortium is a non-profit organization created to ensure the future of UAVCAN and to provide a neutral collaboration platform for the key stakeholders. The core objectives of the Consortium are as follows:

  • It is a governing body that defines the long-term strategy of the project based on the input from the members.
  • It provides legal protection for the project in relevant markets and regions across the world. This includes trademark management and enforcement of the licensing terms.
  • It serves as a testing and certification organization, validating the conformance of hardware and software products to the published UAVCAN standards. Vendors whose products have been shown to be conformant are granted the license to use the official UAVCAN Conformance Mark (shown on the image).
  • It provides consulting services to members, key open source projects, and non-profit organizations leveraging UAVCAN to ensure the long-term health and sustainability of the ecosystem.
  • Top-tier members of the Consortium may opt to list their conformant products (both hardware and software) in the public Conformant Product Directory, which serves as a neutral and unbiased source of information for end system integrators about the COTS products currently available on the market.
  • Top-tier members of the Consortium may propose the inclusion of their vendor-specific DSDL definitions into the public regulated data type repository.

Membership

Benefits

Most importantly, active members help the UAVCAN Consortium sustain the development and maintenance of the technical specifications, reference implementations, key software tools, and other critical components that the entire UAVCAN ecosystem rests upon.

Aside from that, members are also entitled to more direct benefits depending on the membership tier: Sponsored, Regular, or Expert.

Sponsored members are entitled to:

  • Participate in the technical steering and internal strategic goal setting.
  • Directly influence the design and development of technical specifications, standards, and reference implementations published by the UAVCAN Consortium.
  • Access the internal documentation library, which includes technical reports and specifications published by third parties.
  • Take part in surveying the adopter base and get access to the raw results.

Members of the Regular tier are entitled to the following benefits in addition to the above:

  • Submit hardware and software products for conformance testing against the UAVCAN specifications. Conformance testing is performed by the UAVCAN Consortium experts for an additional fee established on a case-by-case basis depending on the complexity of the product and the scope of the tested functionality. This activity also includes consulting services intended to help the vendor reach full conformance with the applicable specifications.
  • Use the UAVCAN Conformance Mark with conformant products.

Members of the Expert tier are entitled to the following benefits in addition to all of the above:

  • List conformant products in the Conformant Product Directory.
  • Add vendor-specific data types to the public regulated data type repository.
  • Access translations of the core technical documentation and specifications in Chinese, Russian, and other languages.

  • an accredited academic institution;
  • a registered non-profit organization;
  • an open source project hosted or mirrored on GitHub, Bitbucket, or GitLab that is starred, watched, or forked by at least 100 users.

Terms

Active members agree to have their logo featured on the official UAVCAN website for the duration of their membership and for up to one month after its termination. If this is undesirable, please communicate your preference to the office.

Membership is auto-renewed annually until explicitly terminated by the member.

When the membership is terminated, the member retains the right to use the official UAVCAN Conformance Mark with products or revisions thereof that have been found conformant previously. The UAVCAN Consortium may choose to de-list all or some of the products submitted by the former member to the Conformant Product Directory at its own discretion. Likewise, the UAVCAN Consortium may choose to deprecate and eventually remove all data types submitted by the former member to the public regulated data types repository, unless doing so is expected to harm the ecosystem.

Please fill out the application form and send it to consortium@uavcan.org. If you have any questions or feedback, please contact the office using the same address.
